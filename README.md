# Feasibility Dossier: Ionic-Core Clock-State Targets in Circular Rydberg Atoms

**Author:** Ulrich Warring, Physikalisches Institut, Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg

## What this is

This repository contains an open feasibility study exploring whether the circular Rydberg state (CRS) platform developed by the Meinert group at the 5. Physikalisches Institut, Universität Stuttgart, could be used for precision measurements of ionic-core atomic-structure quantities relevant to strontium ion clocks.

The study was inspired by a lab visit to the Stuttgart experiment and by the group's published work, in particular:

- Hölzl et al., *Long-Lived Circular Rydberg Qubits of Alkaline-Earth Atoms in Optical Tweezers*, Phys. Rev. X **14**, 021024 (2024)
- Wirth et al., *Quadrupole Coupling of Circular Rydberg Qubits to Inner Shell Excitations*, Phys. Rev. Lett. **133**, 123403 (2024)

The core idea is that the electric-field gradient generated by a circular Rydberg electron at the position of the Sr⁺ ionic core provides a geometrically constrained perturbation that could be used to extract quadrupole moments, E2 matrix elements, and related quantities under electromagnetic boundary conditions qualitatively different from those of a Paul trap.

## What this is not

This is **not** a claim of demonstrated precision, nor a programmatic commitment. It is a feasibility analysis that identifies candidate measurement targets, ranks them by plausibility, specifies falsifiable feasibility gates, and defines a no-go criterion. All quantitative estimates are order-of-magnitude.

The study explicitly concludes that CRS is not a replacement for ion clocks but a potential **differential probe of atomic structure under distinct conditions**. The scientific value lies in orthogonal systematics, not in competing for absolute accuracy.
