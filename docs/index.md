# Ionic-Core Clock-State Targets in Circular Rydberg Atoms

**A feasibility study by Ulrich Warring, Universität Freiburg**

---

## Overview

This open feasibility study asks whether the circular Rydberg state (CRS) platform developed by the Meinert group at Universität Stuttgart could be used for precision measurements of ionic-core atomic-structure quantities relevant to strontium ion clocks.

The central idea: the electric-field gradient generated by a circular Rydberg electron at the Sr⁺ ionic core has a simpler geometric provenance than externally applied trap gradients, and could be used to extract quadrupole moments and related quantities under qualitatively different electromagnetic boundary conditions from a Paul trap.

The study was inspired by a lab visit to the Stuttgart experiment and by the group's published results on quadrupole coupling of circular Rydberg qubits to inner-shell excitations.

---

## Key findings

**High-potential targets (Category A1):**
- Electric quadrupole moment Θ(D₃/₂) of ⁸⁸Sr⁺ — first direct measurement plausible at the 5–10% level
- Electric quadrupole moment Θ(D₅/₂) of ⁸⁸Sr⁺ — potential improvement on the 20-year-old Barwood et al. (2004) value

**Strategic positioning:**
- CRS is not a replacement for ion clocks
- It is a differential probe of atomic structure under distinct electromagnetic boundary conditions
- The scientific value lies in orthogonal systematics, not in competing for absolute accuracy

**Critical bottleneck:**
- A quantitative forward model for the core–Rydberg coupling Hamiltonian, with uncertainty propagation, is required before precision extraction can be attempted
